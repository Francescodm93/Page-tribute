Michael Jackson

The King of pop

Here's a time line of Micheal Jackson:

  • 1958 - Michael Joseph Jackson born in St. Mary's Mercy Hospital of Gary, in India
  • 1964 - Growing up in a family dedicated to music, Michael came into contact with the world of entertainment from an early age. at the age of five, he was aggregated by his father into the group of his brothers, who did not yet have a definitive name. Michael was initially the percussionist, but later began sharing the singing part with his brother Jermaine and the group changed their name to The Jackson 5
  • 1970 - Their debut single I Want You Back reached # 1 on the Billboard Hot 100, Michael became the youngest singer to make it to 1ª on the US charts at the age of 11. With the release of their next three singles, ABC, The Love You Save and I'll Be There, the Jackson 5 became the first artists in history to debut with four consecutive Billboard number one singles
  • 1980 - He won three American Music Awards and a Grammy Award for his performance on Don't Stop 'Til You Get Enough. However, as stated later in his autobiography, despite all this success, Jackson was disappointed with the results obtained from the album, which he said should have had a greater impact
  • 1983 - With the airing of the Billie Jean video, Michael Jackson became the first black artist to be broadcast on MTV
  • 1991 - Jackson signed with Sony Music the most lucrative contract in music history, worth $ 900 million, touted by the media at the time as "the billion dollar contract". On November 26, Dangerous was released, debuting at 1ª on the Billboard 200 and in 10 other countries
  • 1995 - The double album HIStory: Past, Present and Future Book was released which debuted at 1ª on the Billboard 200 and in 19 other countries. The first album, HIStory Begins, was a Greatest Hits of 15 Jackson hits, while the second, HIStory Continues, contained 15 previously unreleased tracks. To date, HIStory has sold 30 million copies (60 million units) worldwide, 8 of which in the United States, making it the best-selling double album of all time
  • 2001 - Jackson gave a humanitarian address at the University of Oxford, where he was also awarded an honorary degree in Literature and Education. He not only became one of the few artists to be featured for the second time, but at 42 years old, he was also the youngest solo artist ever introduced
  • 2009 - Dies in Los Angeles at the age of 50
